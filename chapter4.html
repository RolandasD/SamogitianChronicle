Chapter 4: Storm over the Baltic

1457–1461 — Centralization, the Gotland War, the Lithuanian and Rus’ Campaigns, the Crimean War, and the 1461 Settlements

Prologue: A State of Sinew

On 3 March 1457 the chancery sealed the decrees for a Centralized Monarchical Bureaucracy. Ledgers, musters, tolls, and tides were drawn into one hand. The armies would march the way the clerks wrote: straight, swift, and inevitable.

— Chronicle of Varniai, “On the Year of Papers and Spears”

Centralization gave us reach; fleets would give us presence. With the Royal Navy and Baltic Fleet balanced across Klaipėda and Riga, the court judged that borders were no longer the edge of our ambition but the beginning of our roads.

“If the realm is a blade, let the chancery be its hilt.”
— Sister Agnė of Varniai
