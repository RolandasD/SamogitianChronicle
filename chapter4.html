Chapter 4: Storm over the Baltic

1457–1461 — Centralization, the Gotland War, the Lithuanian and Rus’ Campaigns, the Crimean War, and the 1461 Settlements

Prologue: A State of Sinew

On 3 March 1457 the chancery sealed the decrees for a Centralized Monarchical Bureaucracy. Ledgers, musters, tolls, and tides were drawn into one hand. The armies would march the way the clerks wrote: straight, swift, and inevitable.

— Chronicle of Varniai, “On the Year of Papers and Spears”

Centralization gave us reach; fleets gave us presence. With the Royal Navy and Baltic Fleet keeled in Klaipėda and Riga, borders ceased to be the ends of maps and became the beginnings of roads.

“If the realm is a blade, let the chancery be its hilt.”
— Sister Agnė of Varniai

Peaces and the New Map (1461)

The war-rooms fell silent; the chancery took up the pen. The three great settlements of 1461 redrew the Baltic and the forests of the Rus’.

Crimean Settlement (11 September 1461)

  • Circassia freed: Crimea ends overlordship over Circassia.
  • War reparations: 8.8 ducats to Samogitia.
  • Aggressive Expansion: 0.0 AE; Prestige: Samogitia +0.6, Crimea −0.6.

Pskov–Muscovite Articles (28 September 1461)

  • Cessions: Pskov cedes Pskov and Ostrov to Samogitia.
  • To Novgorod: Muscovy returns cores Totma, Ust-Vym, Vologda, Ustyug.
  • Trade: Muscovy forced to steer trade to Samogitia during truce.
  • Releases: Muscovy releases Yaroslavl, Tver, Ryazan, Rostov, Qasim, Nizhny Novgorod, Belozero (all guaranteed by Samogitia for duration of truce).
  • Overlordship ended: Perm freed from Muscovite suzerainty.
  • Religion: Muscovy converts to Catholic.
  • War reparations: 262.8 ducats to Samogitia.
  • Impact: 1.3 AE; Prestige: Samogitia +12.3, Muscovy −12.3.

Lithuanian Accord (11 November 1461)

  • Cessions to Samogitia: From Odoyev — Yelets, Odoev; From Novgorod — Ingermanland, Neva, Novgorod, Kholm, Luki.
  • To Muscovy: Novgorod returns the core of Velsk to Muscovy.
  • New States: Novgorod releases Karelia and Sápmi (both guaranteed by Samogitia during the truce).
  • War indemnity: 805.0 ducats to Samogitia; inflation +0.01.
  • Impact: 8.8 AE; Prestige: Samogitia +10.2, Lithuania −10.2.
“Power is not merely what you seize in the field, but what you leave standing in the treaty.”
— Chancellor Mykolas of Varniai

Balance of Power — What Changed

  • The Neva Gate: Control of Neva and Novgorod secures Baltic–Inland trade flow; Muscovy is made to steer trade toward Samogitia.
  • Shattered Colossus: A ring of released principalities hems Muscovy; Catholic conversion reshapes its diplomacy.
  • Northern March: Karelia and Sápmi buffer our claims while flying under our protection.
  • Southern Quiet: Crimea yields coin and a vassal; Circassia steps out from Tatar shade.
  • Sea Supremacy: The Gotland annexation and Gulf of Finland victories complete a Samogitian Baltic arch.

Summary: Centralization decrees (3 Mar 1457). Swift victory over Gotland with two June naval actions; full annexation and 30 ducats (1458). Devastating Lithuanian campaign with four set-piece land battles and two Gulf of Finland victories (1459). Rus’ fronts shattered at Ostrov, Moskva, and Rostov (1460). Southern thrust against Crimea and allies with victory at Poltava (1461). Peaces: Crimea (11 Sep — Circassia freed, 8.8 ducats), Pskov–Muscovy (28 Sep — cessions, releases, Catholic conversion, 262.8 ducats, trade steered), Lithuania (11 Nov — Neva/Novgorod belt, Karelia & Sápmi released, 805 ducats, +0.01 inflation). The Baltic order turns to the Samogitian clock.
