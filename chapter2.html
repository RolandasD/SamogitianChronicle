Chapter 2: Iron Wolf

1445–1448 — Naming, expansion, War of the Orders, and elevation to a Kingdom

In June 1445 the field army is formally named Iron Wolf. Doctrine tightens, logistics improve, and plans form to field a second column.

1445 — Iron Wolf Established

Strength rises to 10,000: 5,000 infantry, 3,000 cavalry, 2,000 artillery.

1446 — Expansion & Dynasty

January 1446: a second regimented army, Black Death, is raised under Kantibutas Giedraiciai (OOB 5k/3k/2k).

19 February 1446: an heir, Rolandas, is born (stats 4/2/2). On the same day, a marriage is concluded with Ausra.

26 April 1446: Poland selects a Jagiellon to succeed Władysław III and enters a personal union with Lithuania. Both realms are ruled by Kazimierz Jagiellon (stats 4/4/1), with heir August Jagiellon.

15 May 1446: Samogitia declares Poland as a rival, formalizing the shift from caution to competition in the Vistula basin.

Peace of 28 January 1448

  • Full annexation of the Livonian Order.
  • Riga cedes Riga to Samogitia.
  • Teutonic Order cedes Memel, Insterburg, Königsberg, Ermland, Ortelsburg, Marienburg, Danzig, Tuchel, Kulm, Ostroede to Samogitia.
  • Livonian Order pays 170.0 ducats; loses 150 Splendor.
  • Samogitia gains 9.2 Prestige; suffers 5.1 AE; +0.01 inflation.

28 January 1448: Peace treaty concluded. War ended in decisive victory.

Elevation and Diplomacy (1448)

  • 7 March 1448: Proclaimed the Kingdom of Samogitia.
  • 13 April 1448: Claimed Baltic Cultural Hegemony.
  • 1 June 1448: Entered a Royal Marriage with Brandenburg.

Development Drive (1448–1449)

Throughout 1448 and into 1449, the realm undertook extensive development projects across core provinces.
