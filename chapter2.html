Chapter 2: Iron Wolf

1445–1446 — Naming and expansion

In June 1445, the field army is formally named Iron Wolf. Doctrine tightens, logistics improve, and a second army is planned to spread risk and extend reach.

1445 — Iron Wolf Established

Strength rises to 10,000: 5,000 infantry, 3,000 cavalry, 2,000 artillery.

1446 — Second Army Raised

The second army, Black Death, mirrors Iron Wolf’s composition and enables true dual-column operations: one column screens and raids while the other advances with guns to reduce forts.
