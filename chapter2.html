Chapter 2: Iron Wolf

1445–1448 — Naming, expansion, War of the Orders, and elevation to a Kingdom

Prologue: The Naming, Summer 1445

Cloister bells rolled across the parade square and the banners smelled of fresh dye… Geležinis Vilkas—the Iron Wolf. The name landed like a mailed fist: not boast, but burden.

That afternoon the line wheeled on a single beat of the drum; the dust rose in a perfect crescent…

— Chronicle of Varniai, “On the Banners of the Year 1445”

When the court named our field host Iron Wolf in the summer of 1445, it was more than ceremony. It was a promise…

“Name a thing not for what it is, but for what you demand it become.”
— Sister Agnė of Varniai

1445 — The Naming of the Iron Wolf

The army grew to 10,000 (5k/3k/2k)… Logistics were scripture: rye, oats, salt, and boots—always boots.

1446 — A Second Claw, and a Son

In January 1446 the crown raised Black Death under Kantibutas Giedraiciai 5k/3k/2k. On 19 Feb, heir Rolandas 4/2/2 was born—the day of the royal marriage to Ausra.

26 Apr Poland–Lithuania PU under Kazimierz Jagiellon 4/4/1 (heir: August). 15 May we declared Poland our rival.

Livonian Order fully annexed; Riga and ten Teutonic provinces ceded (Memel, Insterburg, Königsberg, Ermland, Ortelsburg, Marienburg, Danzig, Tuchel, Kulm, Ostroede). +170 ducats; +9.2 prestige; +5.1 AE; +0.01 inflation.
