Prologue

The Founding Legend

In the twilight of the old faith, when the forests of Žemaitija whispered with pagan voices, a child was born in Varniai: Rolandas Drungilas. At fifteen he was raised by the acclaim of elders and the sign of a white bear that bowed before him in the grove of Medvėgalis. From that day the folk named him the Bear‑King.

“The old gods yield their charge, and the young lion shall rule the forest.”

Geography at the Dawn

  • Capital: Varniai
  • Neighbors: Teutonic Order (west), Livonian Order (north), Lithuania (south & east)
  • Land: Forest, marsh and field; rivers Venta and Dubysa
