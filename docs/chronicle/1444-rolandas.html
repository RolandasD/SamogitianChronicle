Chapter I — The Bear‑King

In the Year of Our Lord 1444, Rolandas Drungilas, fifteen years of age, was crowned in Varniai. He was brilliant, unmarried, and without heir; his claim was steadied by omen and by the acclaim of elders.

Chronicle of Varniai
“The crown weighed heavy, though wrought of gold; it bore the burden of youth, of forests, and of foes on every border.”

First Musters

The ducal host was small but keen: the Iron Wolf regiment — 5,000 infantry and 1,000 cavalry — led by Zygimantas Sudimantaitis.

Faith & Rite

Though crowned beneath the Cross, the Bear‑King forbade no village their fires to Perkūnas. Thus began the long balance: altar and oak‑grove.
