Chapter 3: Lion of the North

1449–1453 — Swedish Independence, Baltic wars, Renaissance, and the Imperial Crown

1449 — A Promise to Sweden

15 November 1449 Sweden sought friends for a bid at freedom. Samogitia answered. On 1 December 1449 our banners rode into the War of Swedish Independence, a quiet oath made loud by marching feet.

1450 — Three New Standards

By January 1450 the army broadened from three to six regiments, each 5k/3k/2k:

  • Royal Force — Gintautas Tyzenhaus
  • Pagan Fear — Tarvydas Korybut
  • Iron Legion — Lingvenis Olshanski

They joined Iron Wolf, Black Death, and Green Legion, hardening a true field army of maneuver.

1453 — Peace, Renaissance, and a Crown

17 February 1453: Denmark yielded: ten years of war reparations and 345 ducats; Samogitia gained +2.0 prestige (Denmark −2.0).

1 March 1453: the court embraced the Renaissance. 9 March 1453: noble privileges were curtailed, drawing authority back to the crown. 14 April 1453: amid bells and drums, our sovereigns were crowned Emperor and Empress of Samogitia.

Armies Recast — August 1453

On 18 August 1453, the three veteran corps—Iron Wolf, Black Death, and Green Legion—each expanded to 10k/6k/4k. Field strength by year’s end: 60,000 infantry, 36,000 cavalry, 24,000 artillery.

Shifting Thrones

1 January 1453: Lithuania broke free of the Polish union, with Zygimantas I Mantigirdaitis on the throne—another piece moved on the Baltic board.
