Chapter 1: Dawn over Samogitia

1444 — The first formations

A Soldier’s Note, Winter 1444

We woke to frost on the cart-linens and a red sky over the pines… Tyzenhaus rode the line without a trumpet; only a nod for the captains, and for us a look that measured boots, straps, eyes.

They say great kingdoms live by their banners. We live by our boots. When the Orders boast of their cannon, we sharpen axes and build causeways through marsh that would swallow an army with a single misstep.

Tonight the quartermaster writes that we are six thousand souls. Tomorrow the chaplain will say seven thousand—for he counts the saints. I count the men beside me… and think: if we must be anvil, let us be the one that breaks hammers.

— Anonymous pikeman, copied later into the Book of Campfires

Dawn breaks over pine and peat, over dunes that hold the sea at arm’s length and over river-mist that threads the valleys of Žemaitija… Between larger crowns and older Orders, Samogitia steps into the year 1444 with a small, disciplined heart.

“A young kingdom is a blade of grass in spring—soft in the hand, and yet with the strength to split stone.”
— Chronicle of Varniai, c. 1480

The Land & the People

The heartland is forest-thick and water-latticed… This is the temper of Samogitia—sinew before luxury, duty before display.

Order of Battle, 1444

The standing force totals 6,000 ( 5,000 infantry, 1,000 cavalry, 0 artillery ), commanded by Daugvilas Tyzenhaus.

Doctrine & Drill

Without guns, siege is patience and persuasion… Tyzenhaus’ maxim: “A small army must move as one mind.”

“We will be fewer than those who come for us. Then we must be earlier, steadier, and truer.”
— Daugvilas Tyzenhaus, winter 1443/44

Position & Priorities

Hold the heartland, keep the roads, watch the river mouths, and keep the ledger balanced enough to feed the line through spring mud.

Faces of the Field Army

Tyzenhaus walks the line counting pikeheads as a merchant counts coin… The rankers are sons of foresters and fishermen…

Limits that Shape Us

  • No field artillery: walls are negotiated, not battered.
  • Every casualty counts: retreats are tactical, not shameful.
  • Diplomacy is logistics by other means: stable borders feed the army.

Signals of a Coming Name

Geležinis Vilkas, the Iron Wolf, is already whispered… When banners are stitched, the words will be waiting.
