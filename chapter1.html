Prologue

In 1444, the Duchy of Samogitia stepped out from the shadow of Lithuania with one lonely province—Žemaitija—and a history written against crusaders and orders. On 11 November, a fifteen‑year‑old duke, Rolandas Drungilas, raised a fragile light of sovereignty amidst storms of greater powers. What followed would test resolve, law, and coin alike.

Origins of the Realm

  • Borderland Identity: Samogitia’s marches weathered crusading wars and shifting overlords, shaping a hard, communal resilience.
  • Break from Lithuania: A political fracture in 1444 left a small but determined duchy under a new house.
  • A Young Founder: Rolandas Drungilas, though untested, governs with a reputation for fairness and a keen sense for trade.
  • Perilous Neighbors: The Teutonic and Livonian Orders eye the Baltic border, while Lithuania watches the new duchy’s first steps.
