Chapter 5: The ‘Why Not?’ War

1465–1470 — Curtailing the Clergy, the Dream Fleet, Grand Musters, and the Polish War

1465 — A Tighter Reign & A Third Fleet

On 1 July 1465 the crown curtailed Clergy privileges, tightening revenue and command. That winter a third squadron was signed into being: the Dream Fleet (Gotland), an ocean-minded counterpart to the Royal Navy (Klaipėda) and the Baltic Fleet (Riga).

1466–1467 — Grand Musters

On 17 May 1466 the Pagan Fear and Royal Force were each raised to 32/10/10. By 1467 our banners showed 124/56/44 (I/C/A), a depth that would soon be tested.

1468–1470 — The Lithuanian–Polish ‘Why Not?’ War

On 31 May 1468 Lithuania (the Samogitian crown in a new style) declared war on Poland. “Why not?” was the wry note in the chancery margin; most of Europe answered Poland’s call.

The End of Poland (1470)

By 2 August 1470 the ledger wrote the last line. Poland accepted full annexation, ceded Mazovian towns to Lithuania, and triggered a ring of core returns and releases across the Empire’s west. Indemnities: 570 ducats. Why Not? indeed.
